Advanced technique 03

Give the character a stage.

A background can make a portrait feel finished, but it should stay quiet. Use simple planes, gradients, haze, or faction colors to support the silhouette.

← Advanced OverviewBuild a background ↓
Purpose

Separate the figure without competing with it.

Use it when

A transparent render feels too bare, or a campaign needs faction colors, portrait plates, or a consistent mood.

Do not bother when

The token will sit on many different maps. Keep a transparent master and add the background as a separate version.

Required context

Object Mode, World settings, simple materials, camera view, and basic light placement.

Where it fits: after framing and lighting; before or after post-processing depending on whether the effect should touch the character.
Three simple approaches

Start with the least complicated option.

World color

  1. Open World Properties and choose a dark gray or colored background.
  2. Lower strength until it supports the silhouette without flattening the light.
  3. Render once with Film → Transparent and once without, so you keep both choices.

Background card

  1. Add a plane behind the character and scale it larger than the camera view.
  2. Move it with G; keep it behind every visible part of the figure.
  3. Give it a simple material: solid color, gradient, or a low-contrast noise texture.

Glow / haze

  1. Place a large plane behind the figure with a darker base color.
  2. Add a soft colored Area Light or emission circle behind the head / torso.
  3. Use fog or compositor glare only after the silhouette is already clear.

Transparent versus rendered: transparent is the flexible master; a rendered background is a finished presentation. Export both when you are unsure.
VTT check

Let the silhouette keep the microphone.

Contrast

Make the background value different from the outer edge of the figure.

Cropping

Keep the glow or plate inside the same crop so it does not feel accidentally cut off.

Common mistake

Adding environmental detail that is beautiful at 100% but becomes visual static at token size.
