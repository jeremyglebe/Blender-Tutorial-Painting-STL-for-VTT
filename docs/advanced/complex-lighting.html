Advanced technique 02

Light the part that matters.

Move beyond “the model is visible” into deliberate portrait lighting. Key, fill, rim, color, and emission can make the face, weapon, or magical detail lead the eye.

← Advanced OverviewSee lighting recipes ↓
Purpose

Give the render a mood and a focal point.

Use it when

The basic Sun + Area setup is readable but generic, or when a villain, hero, monster, or magical character needs a distinct mood.

Do not bother when

The face and silhouette are still hard to read. More lights will not fix a bad camera or muddy material separation.

Required context

Object Mode and Rendered View. Know how to add, move, rotate, rename, and select lights.

Where it fits: after basic camera and materials; tune this before AO, Freestyle, and final post-processing.
Build the rig

Add lights by job.

  1. Add or keep a low-strength Sun as broad visibility. Rename it KEY_SUN if you keep it.
  2. Add an Area Light as the key. Move with G, rotate with R, and place it front / above / side.
  3. Add a weaker fill from the camera side only if the shadow side disappears. Name it FILL_SOFT.
  4. Add a rim light behind and to one side. Give it a color that supports the character, not one that fights the palette.
  5. For eyes, runes, or magic, make an emission material and add a small Point or Area Light near the source to fake spill.

Name everything

Clear names such as KEY_WARM, FILL_COOL, RIM_RED, and RUNE_SPILL turn an intimidating Outliner into a control panel.

Starting recipes

Change one mood at a time.

Basic portrait

  • Low Sun for visibility
  • Warm Area key, front / above / side
  • AO on
  • Neutral or transparent background

Dramatic villain

  • Very low Sun or no Sun
  • Strong side Area key
  • Dark background
  • Red / orange rim light and stronger shadows

Heroic fantasy

  • Warm key light
  • Cool rim light
  • Soft fill for face readability
  • Bright weapon or face value

Magical character

  • Normal key / fill
  • Emission eyes or runes
  • Small colored light near the glow source
  • Optional bloom / glare later

Horror / monster

  • Low fill
  • Harsh side light or underlight
  • Dark background
  • Strong silhouette and limited visible color
VTT check

Guide the eye, do not hide the token.

Priority

Eyes, face, weapon, and silhouette matter most. Let the brightest or highest-contrast area support one of those.

Common mistake

A colored rim that is so bright it becomes a second character. Lower power before changing the color.

Test

Render a tiny preview. If the face vanishes, add fill or move the key before adding more effects.
