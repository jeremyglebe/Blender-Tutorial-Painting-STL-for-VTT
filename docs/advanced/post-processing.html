Advanced technique 04

Polish the render, quietly.

Post-processing is the last readability pass: contrast, color, glow, shadow, stroke, and downscaling. Keep it subtle enough that the miniature still feels like the source.

← Advanced OverviewStart a polish pass ↓
Purpose

Make the finished image survive its final size.

Use it when

The render is close, but the face is flat, the edge disappears, or glow / shadow needs a final nudge.

Do not bother when

You are still changing the camera, materials, or lighting. Fix upstream decisions before polishing.

Required context

Render Result, Compositor basics, and output resolution. GIMP, Krita, Photopea, Photoshop, or Affinity Photo are optional later.

Where it fits: after the render is readable. Post-processing should be a controlled finishing pass, not a rescue operation.
Blender-first pass

Use the Compositor as a small finishing desk.

  1. Render a transparent PNG first. Keep the clean version before adding effects.
  2. Open the Compositing workspace and enable Use Nodes.
  3. Connect Render Layers through a Color Balance or RGB Curves node for contrast and color.
  4. Add Glare in Fog Glow mode for eyes, runes, or magical highlights. Keep threshold and mix conservative.
  5. Use a second branch for a soft shadow / halo only if the output background needs separation.
  6. Preview at the final VTT size, then save the composited result and the clean master.

Simple effects menu

  • Contrast: Color Balance / RGB Curves
  • Color mood: Color Balance
  • Glow: Glare / Fog Glow
  • Edge support: outer stroke or silhouette branch
  • Final crispness: light sharpening after downscale
  • Atmosphere: vignette or soft background wash
Effect order

One effect, one reason.

EffectGood useStop when
Contrast bumpFace and major material zones feel dullDark areas lose sculpt detail
Color balancePush a warm tavern or cold moonlit moodSkin / bone colors look unnatural
Bloom / glowMake emission feel magicalThe glow hides the rune or face
Halo / shadowSeparate transparent character from a busy mapIt looks like a sticker outline
SharpeningRecover crispness after downscaleEdges become crunchy or noisy

VTT check

Downscale before you decide.

Preview small

View the final token at the size it will appear in play. Effects that look elegant large may become a blob.

Keep masters

Save the transparent render and the composited render separately. You will want the clean version later.

Common mistake

Stacking glow, vignette, stroke, and sharpening because each looked good alone.
