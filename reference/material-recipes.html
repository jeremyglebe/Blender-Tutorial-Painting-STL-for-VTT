Material cookbook

Start with a readable surface.

These are starting-point material recipes for STL-to-VTT renders. They are not physically perfect shader setups; they are meant to produce attractive, readable art quickly. Adjust for the model, lighting, and final camera angle.

← ReferenceJump to recipes ↓
Before you tune

Use the same material in two views.

While painting

Judge colors in Material Preview so you can see broad zones without waiting for a full render.

Before export

Check again in Rendered View with the actual lights, AO, Freestyle, and orthographic camera.

Surface matters

The Surface setting can change how a material reacts to light. Most materials can start with Principled BSDF, but special surfaces are worth testing.

Hair discovery: the Hair surface / default made red hair look dramatically better in one successful render. Try it for mohawks, beards, fur, and hair masses when plain materials look plastic—but do not treat it as a universal rule.
Recipes

Pick a starting point, then render.

Primer / Matte Black Base

Use for: the first/default material on an STL, unpainted areas, dark recesses, and a miniature-painting-style starting point.

SurfaceDiffuse or matte surface
Base ColorVery dark gray, almost black
Metallic0
Roughness.8–1.0 if using Principled BSDF

Notes: naming it Primer or Primer (Matte Black) keeps the material list clear. It makes missed spots look intentional.

Avoid: pure black if it destroys detail; glossy primer unless intentionally using a shiny undercoat.

Skin

Use for: human skin, monster skin, exposed flesh, faces, hands.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorCharacter-appropriate skin tone
Metallic0
Roughness.45–.75

Optional: subtle variation; darker scars; faint red / purple around nose, ears, knuckles, and wounds.

Avoid: overly glossy skin unless wet or slimy; letting the face get too dark.

Fur

Use for: animals, beastmen, fur trim, monster hides.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF or Hair-style surface
Base ColorDark-to-mid fur tone
Metallic0
Roughness.65–.9

Optional: darker recesses, lighter tips, subtle variation, Hair experiments when fur looks flat.

Avoid: shiny fur or noise that becomes static at token size.

Hair / Beards

Use for: hair, mohawks, beards, mustaches, braids.

SurfacePrincipled first; try Hair-style surface / shader
Base ColorChosen hair color
Metallic0
Roughness.5–.85

Optional: darker roots, lighter highlights, stylized color, subtle variation, rim light for silhouette.

Avoid: pure black without highlights; bright red / orange without shadow; messy linework.

Cloth

Use for: tunics, robes, pants, cloaks, banners, wraps.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorChosen cloth color
Metallic0
Roughness.65–.95

Optional: desaturate slightly; darken hems; add subtle variation; paint patterns or stains later.

Avoid: shine unless silk / satin; saturating every cloth zone equally.

Leather

Use for: belts, straps, boots, gloves, pouches, armor padding.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorBrown, black, tan, red-brown, or gray-brown
Metallic0
Roughness.5–.85

Optional: subtle color noise, darker straps, lighter worn edges, lower roughness for polished leather.

Avoid: making every leather piece the same brown or glossy plastic.

Steel / Clean Metal

Use for: weapons, armor, buckles, chains.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorDark gray to light gray
Metallic1.0
Roughness.25–.55

Optional: slight roughness noise, darker metal, brighter edge highlights, Freestyle for shape.

Avoid: mirror polish unless intentional; pure white everywhere; Metallic at 0.

Dark Iron / Blackened Metal

Use for: crude weapons, black armor, Chaos gear, chains, industrial fantasy.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorVery dark gray, not pure black
Metallic1.0
Roughness.4–.75

Optional: rust tint, roughness variation, cool rim light.

Avoid: pure black that loses detail or enough rust to stop reading as metal.

Bronze / Brass / Gold

Use for: jewelry, trim, decorative weapons, relics, holy symbols.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorBronze: reddish orange · Brass: yellow-brown · Gold: warm yellow-orange
Metallic1.0
Roughness.25–.55

Optional: darker recess material nearby; use as a sparing accent.

Avoid: neon yellow or gold on every surface.

Bone / Horn / Teeth / Claws

Use for: horns, tusks, skulls, teeth, claws, trophies.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorIvory, tan, yellowed white, gray-white
Metallic0
Roughness.5–.85

Optional: darker roots, lighter tips, warm recess variation, AO for cracks.

Avoid: sterile pure white or glossy bone unless wet / polished.

Wood

Use for: hafts, staffs, shields, clubs, bows, bases, props.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorMedium, gray, reddish, or warm brown
Metallic0
Roughness.55–.9

Optional: procedural noise, darker stripes, lighter worn edges, texture-painted grain later.

Avoid: making every wood piece orange or identical.

Stone / Base

Use for: rocks, bases, ruins, statues, masonry.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorGray, tan, dark brown, green-gray
Metallic0
Roughness.75–1.0

Optional: procedural noise, moss tint, dark cracks, strong AO.

Avoid: shiny stone unless wet or a base color that competes with the character.

Blood / Gore

Use for: wounds, weapon blood, gore details, horror monsters.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorDark red, brown-red, black-red
Metallic0
Roughness.2–.55

Optional: lower roughness for fresh blood; higher roughness for dried blood.

Avoid: pure bright red or so much blood that the model becomes noise.

Glowing Eyes

Use for: undead, demons, magic users, constructs, mutations.

SurfaceEmission or emission-enabled material
EmissionDesired glow color · start strength 1–3
MetallicNot needed
RoughnessNot the main control

Optional: small colored light, compositor bloom / glare, darker surrounding face.

Avoid: a glow so large it hides the face or clips everything to white.

Runes / Magic / Fire / Warpstone

Use for: magical weapons, runes, gems, spell effects, glowing cracks, weirdstone.

SurfaceEmission or emission-enabled material
EmissionModerate strength to start
ColorsGreen, blue, orange, yellow, purple, or pink by effect
MetallicNot the main control

Optional: matching Point / Area Light, compositor glare, faint reflected color painted nearby.

Avoid: making every magical element equally bright or blooming away its form.

Mutated / Diseased Flesh

Use for: Chaos mutations, undead skin, infected wounds, monster flesh.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base ColorGray-green, purple-red, sickly yellow, pale gray
Metallic0
Roughness.45–.8

Optional: painted mottling, dark lesions, glossy wet patches, supernatural emission.

Avoid: color noise everywhere or losing the main silhouette.

General rules

Small values, big readability.

  • Pure black loses detail.
  • Pure white blows out quickly.
  • Roughness controls matte versus glossy response.
  • Metallic is usually 0 or 1, not a random middle value.
  • Lighting changes materials dramatically.
  • Test through the final camera view.
  • VTT art favors readability over realism.
  • Subtle variation beats noisy variation.

Surface reminder: if hair looks plastic, try a Hair-style surface / shader. If cloth shines, raise roughness. If metal looks gray, set Metallic to 1.0 before adding complexity.

Common mistakes

Fix the visual symptom.

ProblemFirst fix
Metal looks like gray plasticSet Metallic to 1.0 and adjust roughness.
Hair looks like plasticTry a Hair-style surface / shader, add variation, and improve the light.
Cloth looks shinyIncrease roughness.
Bone looks too cleanUse ivory / tan and a darker recess tone.
Glow is invisibleIncrease emission strength or darken the surroundings.
Glow is blown outLower emission strength or reduce bloom / glare.
Everything looks flatImprove lighting and AO before rebuilding every material.
