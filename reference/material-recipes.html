Material cookbook

Start with a readable surface.

These are starting-point material recipes for STL-to-VTT renders. They are not physically perfect shader setups; they are meant to produce attractive, readable art quickly. Adjust for the model, lighting, and final camera angle.

← ReferenceJump to recipes ↓
Before tuning

Use the same material in two views.

While painting

Judge colors in Material Preview to see broad zones without waiting for a full render.

Before export

Check again in Rendered View with the actual lights, AO, Freestyle, and orthographic camera.

Surface matters

The Surface setting can change how a material reacts to light. Most materials can start with Principled BSDF, but special surfaces are worth testing.

Hair discovery: the Hair surface / default made red hair look dramatically better in one successful render. It is worth testing for mohawks, beards, fur, and hair masses when plain materials look plastic, but it is not a universal rule.

Primer / Matte Black Base

Use for: the first/default material on an STL, unpainted areas, dark recesses, and a miniature-painting-style starting point.

SurfaceDiffuse or matte surface
Base Color
Metallic0
Roughness.8–1.0 if using Principled BSDF

Notes: naming it Primer or Primer (Matte Black) keeps the material list clear. It makes missed spots look intentional.

Avoid: pure black if it destroys detail; glossy primer unless intentionally using a shiny undercoat.

Skin

Use for: human skin, monster skin, exposed flesh, faces, hands.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF
Base Color
Metallic0
Roughness.45–.75

Optional: subtle variation; darker scars; faint red / purple around nose, ears, knuckles, and wounds.

Avoid: overly glossy skin unless wet or slimy; letting the face get too dark.

Fur

Use for: animals, beastmen, fur trim, monster hides.

SurfacePrincipled BSDF or Hair-style surface
Base Color
Metallic0
Roughness.65–.9

Optional: darker recesses, lighter tips, subtle variation, Hair experiments when fur looks flat.

Avoid: shiny fur or noise that becomes static at token size.

Hair / Beards

Use for: hair, mohawks, beards, mustaches, braids.

Base Color
Metallic0
Roughness.5–.85

Optional: darker roots, lighter highlights, stylized color, subtle variation, rim light for silhouette.

Avoid: pure black without highlights; bright red / orange without shadow; messy linework.

General rules

Small values, big readability.

  • Pure black loses detail.
  • Pure white blows out quickly.
  • Roughness controls matte versus glossy response.
  • Metallic is usually 0 or 1, not a random middle value.
  • Lighting changes materials dramatically.
  • Test through the final camera view.
  • VTT art favors readability over realism.
  • Subtle variation beats noisy variation.

Surface reminder: if hair looks plastic, try a Hair-style surface / shader. If cloth shines, raise roughness. If metal looks gray, set Metallic to 1.0 before adding complexity.

Common mistakes

Fix the visual symptom.

ProblemFirst fix
Metal looks like gray plasticSet Metallic to 1.0 and adjust roughness.
Hair looks like plasticTry a Hair-style surface / shader, add variation, and improve the light.
Cloth looks shinyIncrease roughness.
Bone looks too cleanUse ivory / tan and a darker recess tone.
Glow is invisibleIncrease emission strength or darken the surroundings.
Glow is blown outLower emission strength or reduce bloom / glare.
Everything looks flatImprove lighting and AO before rebuilding every material.
